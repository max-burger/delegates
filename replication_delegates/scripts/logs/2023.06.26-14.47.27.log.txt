. /* 
> How to treat the shifting variables [large outliers...]
> 
>         A) Log. of shifting to other countries / future
> To reduce outliers, it could be helpful to use logarithms. However, the differ
> ence is negative in some cases. Therefore, taking the logarithm after taking t
> he difference is not straightforward. Calculating the logarithm beforehand and
>  taking differences between logaritzmized values does not work! (I tried :) ->
>  values do not fit when plotted against diff of unlogarithmized values)
> Solutions namend:
> I)  Take log of absolute values and add sign after
> II) Add minimum value [arbitrary number] => For robustness could also try this
> !
> 
> https://www.statalist.org/forums/forum/general-stata-discussion/general/625369
> -log-transformation-of-negative-values
> https://www.researchgate.net/post/How-can-I-log-transform-a-series-with-both-p
> ositive-and-negative-values
> 
>         B) Proportional to own 
> Another possibility would be to use shares: In comparison to the price suggest
> ed to be payed by own country, how much smaller/bigger is the price suggested 
> to be payed by others [this could get rid of huge numbers if they are given pr
> obortionally]:
>         Share: price home / price others
>         Problem: how to deal with 0s in denominator when nominator not 0 ? (if
>  both 0 proportion = 1)
> 
> 
>         C) Binaries
> Shifting to other countries / to future yes/no
> 
> */

. *A) LOGs
. foreach var in shift_to_oecd shift_to_ldc shift_ldc_oecd shift_future_home shi
> ft_future_oecd shift_future_ldc {
  2.     gen ln_`var' = .
  3.         replace ln_`var' = ln(`var' + 1) if `var' >= 0
  4.         replace ln_`var' = ln((`var' - 1) * (-1)) * (-1) if `var' < 0
  5. }
